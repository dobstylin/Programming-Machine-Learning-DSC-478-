Decision Tree and Naive Bayes Analysis¶

The decision tree classification found a high accuracy on the testing set of 98%, while the training set saw an accuracy of only 56%. On the Naive Bayes analysis, A 61% accuracy was seen on the training set, and a 59% accuracy was recorded for the testing set.¶

Bias and Variance¶

Throughout the classification models with weighted distances, high variance is shown throughout by the pattern of high accuracy during the training set and low accuracy in the testing sets. This can be caused by paying too close attention to all movement during the training set and overfitting the model.¶

Both the classification and Naive Bayes models seem to show high bias due to a high error rating on both the training and testing sets. When there is high bias, the model tends to pay less attention to the training sets and this can result in high errors in both training and testing sets due to underfitting.¶
